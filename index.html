A Wave of Gun Violence Hits the South Bronx Amid COVID-19 Pandemic

An eight-year-old shot in her mother’s NYCHA Patterson Housing apartment in Mott Haven. A man shot dead days after being released from prison in Hunts Point. Two women hit by stray bullets outside Mott Haven’s Mill Brook Houses. These incidents are among the 75 shooting victims recorded this year through September 27 by the Police Department City of New York’s 40th and 41st Precinct. The number of shootings rose over two times more compared to the same time period last year, according to NYPD CompStat reports.

Here's what you need to know:

A CompStat report provided by the Police Department City of New York's 40th Precinct recorded a jump in shooting victims from 46 to 51 between September 21 through September 27.

The 40th Precinct Police Department City of New York also recorded 17 shooting incidents between September 21 through September 27. In the same time period last year, 12 shooting incidents were reported to the Police Department.

One of the recent shooting victims included an 8-year-old girl who was shot in the stomach at her mother’s NYCHA Patterson Houses apartment in Mott Haven on Wednesday, September 23.

Two shootings have been reported this year at NYCHA's Patterson Houses, according to Police Officials.
